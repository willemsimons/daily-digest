It trails Claude Fable 5 and GPT 5.6 Sol overall but benchmarks at frontier level on coding, and full weights drop July 27. Making things

What I Learned Selling 2,500 MIDI Recorders
Chip Weinberger

Weinberger's core finding after taking Jamcorder from idea to 2,500 units: the hardware wasn't the hard part — 200,000 lines of firmware, app, and manufacturing tooling across three years (pre-LLM) was. He argues hardware's reputation for difficulty is systematically overstated, and that the saying 'hardware is hard' functions more as a social filter than an accurate description. The kind of obsessive single-thing account that recalibrates how you think about what's actually blocking you.

The Scariest Chart in Electrical Engineering
Veritasium

Veritasium does what he does best: takes a deeply counterintuitive visual artifact — the Smith Chart, which compresses complex RF impedance math onto a single circle — and reveals why engineers use it constantly without fully understanding what it's actually representing geometrically. Worth watching just for the moment the circular mapping clicks.

For you

FILM
Sans Soleil
Chris Marker

The film that basically invented the essay-film as a form — Marker drifts between Tokyo, Guinea-Bissau, and Iceland narrating memory, ritual, and how images construct reality rather than record it. The passage on vertigo and false memory alone will make you want to build something. Pure rabbit hole.

ART
Cybernetic Serendipity: The Computer and the Arts (1968)
Jasia Reichardt / ICA London

The exhibition that first showed the world generative, algorithmic, and computer-made art as a legitimate art scene — before the vocabulary existed. Studying the original catalog is less art history and more a blueprint for exactly the intersection of expressive systems and playful surfaces Willem works in. The ICA documentation is the closest living record.

MUSIC
Hecker / Roden — Sand [2000, 2015 remaster]
Florian Hecker & Steve Roden

Two artists who went unreasonably deep on a narrow thing: Hecker on psychoacoustic synthesis, Roden on found-sound and 'small sound' composition. The collaboration sounds like watching something geological happen in accelerated time — deep structure made audible, beautiful and strange. Perfect for a long making session.
